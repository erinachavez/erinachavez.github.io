History

The 38 flower remedies (plus the crisis formula) were discovered in the 1920s and 30s by Dr. Edward Bach, an English physician and homeopath who wanted to find a gentler form of medicine. He left behind a complete and simple system of healing as gift to all who would like to learn to use it. To learn more about Dr. Edward Bach and his legacy, please visit www.bachcentre.com.

Why I Am a Bach Flower Remedies Practitioner

I have always been interested in self-care, which is available on many levels of the physical, mental, emotional and spiritual. Self-care is not selfish; it is the way that we can be the best versions of ourselves and truly care for others.

I first tried the Bach flowers in the late 90s while working in a spa as an esthetician. I was drawn to the idea that flower drops could address specific emotions, and I was skeptical. Having done a good amount of personal work in prior years, I was expanding my idea of what it meant to be healthy. I decided to try Star of Bethlehem to work through old trauma, and it was transformative for me. I have been working with the Bach flowers ever since, and became a practitioner in 2012. I am currently training to teach Bach Basics, a Bach Foundation approved, introductory course available in the United States. In 2017 I will begin a graduate program in somatic studies.

By taking the step of working with the remedies, we take responsibility for ourselves and our current state, and gently remove obstacles to our own wellbeing. In all the work I do, I hope that people will experience acceptance, empowerment, love, and inner freedom, and that the remedies will help many along this path.
